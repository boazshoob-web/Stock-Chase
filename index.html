Technical Indicators Glossary

SMA 20
Simple Moving Average over 20 periods. Represents the average closing price of the last 20 candles. Used to identify short-term trend direction. When price is above SMA 20, the short-term trend is bullish; below it, bearish.
SMA 50
Simple Moving Average over 50 periods. A medium-term trend indicator. A "Golden Cross" (SMA 50 crossing above SMA 200) is a strong bullish signal; a "Death Cross" (crossing below) is bearish.
SMA 200
Simple Moving Average over 200 periods. The most widely watched long-term trend indicator. Institutional investors often use it to determine the overall market direction. Price above SMA 200 suggests a long-term uptrend.
Bollinger Bands
A volatility indicator consisting of three lines: a middle SMA 20 band and upper/lower bands at 2 standard deviations. When bands squeeze (narrow), a big price move is expected. Price touching the upper band may indicate overbought conditions; touching the lower band may indicate oversold.
Trend Line
A linear regression line fitted to closing prices over the displayed period. Shows the overall price direction and momentum. A positive slope indicates an uptrend; negative indicates a downtrend. The steeper the slope, the stronger the trend.
Support & Resistance (S/R)
Key price levels where buying or selling pressure has historically been strong. Support is a price floor where demand tends to prevent further decline. Resistance is a price ceiling where supply tends to prevent further rise. Breakouts above resistance or below support often lead to significant price moves.
